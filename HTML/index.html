Shreya Misha

CEO & Co-Founder of Flyrobe

Flyrobe, the on-demand wardrobe service founded in October 2015, has created a niche for itself in the four years since its inception, and raised over $7 million from IDG Ventures, Sequoia Capital, PayTM, and several angel investors. The credit for making this business model successful in a society where hand-me-downs, or used clothes, are not considered exciting prospects, goes to Mishra and her co-founders, Pranay Surana and Tushar Saxena.

Raised in a family of IAS officers and lawyers, Mishra never thought she’d venture into the world of business. However, her work experience as a financial analyst gave her insights into the market, the resources it takes to run a business, and got her interested in developing a model that was relatively unexplored. Having always followed fashion trends, Mishra identified a big void in the fashion rental space. “There are several people who invest in expensive clothes, but don’t get many opportunities to wear them.

Today, Flyrobe is reportedly six times bigger than any other fashion rental players in the market. “Any model worth its salt will have multiple tiers. Since rental is a new concept, the more we keep at it, the more consumers will start to understand its features,” a hopeful Mishra says.

Anupriya Kapur

A Single mother & social media consultant

“Being a single mother in India is like being a Unicorn,” says Anupriya Kapur, mom to an 11-year-old boy in Gurugram. She separated from her partner on amicable terms and is now raising her son alone.

A successful entrepreneur and social media consultant, she says that it is not easy being a single parent in this country.“When I walk around in my society, people look at me like it’s a crime for me to be happy,” she says.

Being a single parent, it becomes very crucial to keep the communication channel open with your child(ren), she suggests. Taking them into confidence and explaining things to them in an age-appropriate manner. Doing this will not only make the process a tad bit smoother but will also help in the long run, concludes Anupriya.
